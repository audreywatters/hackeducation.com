Despite the arguments Nicholas Carr makes in his essay "Experiments in delinkification," I think that hyperlinked texts and digital stories are rich and valid forms of expression.

I wrote some of my thoughts about Carr's argument here.

And here, I'd like to list a few recommended stories that, I think, demonstrate the innovation and creativity that electronic literature can offer:

  • Inanimate Alice
  • JB Wock
  • The Jew's Daughter
  • Roulette
  • slippingglimpse
  • Sydney's Syberia

    • You can find more great examples at the Electronic Literature Directory
